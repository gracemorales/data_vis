Interacting with Data

Relationship Between Voting Frequency and Education/Income Level

This parallel categories plot shows the relationship between voting frequency and education/income levels. The plot shows that people who rarely/never vote are more likely to have a high school or less level of education and be in the less than $40k income category. People who always or sporadically vote are more likely to be in the $75k and above income categories.

Voter Turnout and Absentee Voting in Virginia from 2014 to 2022

This bubble chart shows how the percentage of absentee ballots and the percentage of voter turnout changed in Virginia overtime. It seems as though voter turnout increases in the years where there is a presidential election. Absentee voting increased in most Virginia counties in 2020, likely due to the coronavirus pandemic. In 2022, absentee voting overall was still higher than it had been in 2014, 2016, and 2018, even though it was two year after the start of the pandemic.

Virginia Voter Turnout by Locality

This map shows the percent voter turnout by county Virginia. Northern Virginia and Eastern Virginia have the highest voter turnout, while Southwest Virginia has the lowest voter turnout. Voter turnout does not go above 67 percent or below 33 percent. Buchanan County had the lowest voter turnout.
